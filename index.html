What's your cognitive function stack?

A Thinking · Feeling · Intuiting · Sensing test, built on Jung's cognitive functions and the modern function-stack model.

What this measures. Carl Jung described four mental functions — Sensing, Intuiting, Thinking, Feeling — each turned either outward (extraverted) or inward (introverted). That makes eight functions. Everyone uses all eight, but in a personal order of preference — your "stack": a dominant, an auxiliary, a tertiary, and an inferior.

This test scores all eight, then matches you to the best-fitting of the 16 valid stacks — so you can never get an impossible result — and reports your confidence plus the runner-up if it's a close call.

How to answer honestly — this is what makes it accurate:

  • Answer as you actually are, not as you'd like to be or how you act at work.
  • Go with your first instinct; don't overthink any single item.
  • Think across your whole life, not just this week's mood.
  • Use the strong ends when something really fits — or really doesn't.
